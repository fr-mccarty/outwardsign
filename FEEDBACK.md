# User Feedback & Evaluation

This document contains feedback on Outward Sign from the perspective of our key user personas. Use this to guide messaging, feature development, and user experience improvements.

---

## Homepage Evaluation

**Date:** 2025-11-10
**Evaluated By:** Fr. Michael Thompson (Parish Priest) and Maria Rodriguez (Pastoral Associate)
**Page:** Landing page (src/app/page.tsx)

---

## Fr. Michael Thompson's Evaluation

### What Would Resonate with Him ✅

**Strong pain point identification:**
- "Stop juggling scattered documents, endless email chains, and last-minute scrambling" - This is literally his daily experience at St. Joseph's
- The emphasis on having scripts "printed and ready in a binder for the priest" shows they understand exactly what he needs

**Budget concerns immediately addressed:**
- "Free forever • No credit card required • No hidden fees" appears THREE times - this directly addresses his chronic budget anxiety
- The entire "Free & Open Source" section speaks to his financial constraints

**Simplicity promise:**
- Three-step process (Plan → Prepare → Print) feels manageable
- "One-click PDF generation" sounds effortless
- Direct call-out: "Priests & Deacons: Celebrate confidently with complete, print-ready scripts"

### His Concerns and Hesitations ⚠️

**Skepticism about complexity:**
- *"This looks comprehensive, but is it actually simple? I don't have time to learn complicated software."*
- *"There are a lot of features listed - will Maria and I get overwhelmed during setup?"*

**Implementation anxiety:**
- *"How long will it take to get this running? I can't afford weeks of disruption."*
- *"Will this create MORE work initially while we're transitioning from our current system?"*

**Trust issues:**
- *"It says it's free forever, but there must be a catch. What am I missing?"*
- *"Is anyone actually using this? I don't see any testimonials from other priests."*

### Persuasiveness: **6/10 - Interested but Cautious**

He'd likely forward this to Maria with a note: *"What do you think? Looks promising but I want to make sure it won't be another headache."*

---

## Maria Rodriguez's Evaluation

### What Would Resonate with Her ✅

**Detail and control features:**
- "Complete, properly formatted liturgical scripts with readings, prayers, and all celebration details" - This is her dream come true
- "Professional Typography" - She cares deeply about this
- The example wedding script with lector names, pericope references, response text - this level of detail shows they understand

**Organizational capabilities:**
- "From initial planning to the printed script in the sacristy—manage every aspect" - Complete oversight
- "Track participants and family details" - Nothing falls through the cracks
- "Organize readings and liturgical elements" - All her obsessive planning in one place

**Quality emphasis:**
- "Beautiful celebrations are evangelization" - This resonates with her sense of vocation
- The catechism quote shows theological grounding
- "Properly formatted for easy reading during liturgy" - They care about excellence like she does

### Her Concerns and Anxieties ⚠️

**Control and customization:**
- *"Can I adjust the templates to match our parish's exact style? What if the automated formatting isn't quite right?"*
- *"Will I be able to edit scripts manually if needed, or am I locked into templates?"*

**Reliability fears:**
- *"What happens if the website goes down the morning of a funeral?"*
- *"Can I export backups? I need to know I won't lose everything."*

**Collaboration concerns:**
- *"It says 'staff collaboration' - does that mean other people can change my carefully prepared scripts? Can I control who edits what?"*
- *"What if someone on the team makes a mistake that I don't catch?"*

**Transition anxiety:**
- *"I have eight years of data in my current system. How do I migrate everything without losing details?"*
- *"Will this replace ALL my tools, or will I still need my backup spreadsheets? I can't trust a single system."*

### Persuasiveness: **7/10 - Excited but Nervous**

She'd probably create a detailed pros/cons list, visit the site multiple times, and want to see a demo before committing. The features are exactly what she needs, but she'll need reassurance about maintaining her level of control and quality.

## Recommended Improvements Based on Persona Feedback

### 1. **Add Social Proof** (Critical for Fr. Michael)

Add a testimonials section:
- "Fr. James from Holy Family Parish: 'Set up our first wedding in 20 minutes. Now I actually have time to meet with couples instead of formatting documents.'"
- "Maria S., Pastoral Associate: 'Finally, a system that matches my attention to detail. Everything I need is in one place.'"

### 2. **Show, Don't Just Tell Simplicity** (Critical for Fr. Michael)

- Add a short video or animated GIF showing the three steps in action
- Include screenshots of the actual interface with captions like "Clean, simple forms" and "No clutter, just what you need"
- Add timing: "Create your first wedding in under 30 minutes"

### 3. **Address Control & Customization** (Critical for Maria)

Add a feature highlight:

**"Complete Control, When You Need It"**
- Edit any auto-generated script
- Customize templates to your parish's style
- Set permission levels for team collaboration
- Export backups anytime (PDF, Word, or full data export)

### 4. **Reliability & Backup Assurances** (Critical for Maria)

Add to features or FAQ:
- "Your data is automatically backed up daily"
- "Export offline copies of scripts anytime"
- "Works offline - access printed PDFs even without internet"

### 5. **Time Investment Transparency** (Both)

Add to "How It Works" section:
- Setup: 15 minutes to create your parish
- First event: 20-30 minutes
- Subsequent events: 10-15 minutes (reuse readings, templates)

### 6. **Migration Support** (Especially Maria)

Add a section:

**"Moving from Your Current System"**
- Start fresh or import existing events
- Copy/paste readings and prayers
- No pressure - keep your old files as backup while you transition

### 7. **Collaboration Controls Explanation** (Maria's anxiety)

Clarify under "Staff & Family Collaboration":
- "You control who can view, edit, or manage each event"
- "Role-based permissions ensure appropriate access"
- "Audit log shows who made what changes and when"

### 8. **Address the "What's the Catch?" Question** (Fr. Michael)

Add a brief FAQ or sidebar:

**"Why is this free?"**

"We believe every parish deserves excellent sacrament preparation tools, regardless of budget. This is our service to the Catholic community, built by developers who care about the Church. Open source means transparent, community-supported, and free forever."

### 9. **Demonstrate Theological Understanding** (Both)

The catechism quote is good, but add:
- "Built with input from priests, deacons, and pastoral associates"
- "Respects liturgical norms and traditions"
- "Supports both Ordinary and Extraordinary Forms" (if applicable)

### 10. **Add a "Common Questions" Section**

Address:
- "Is this really free forever?" → Yes, explanation
- "What if I need help?" → Support options
- "Can I try it before committing?" → Guest/demo mode
- "What if my parish has unique needs?" → Customization options

---

## Summary

**Fr. Michael** needs reassurance that this won't add to his already overwhelming workload. He needs to see that it's truly simple, that others are successfully using it, and that the setup won't consume his limited time.

**Maria** needs to know she can maintain her level of control and quality. She needs reassurance about reliability, customization, backups, and the ability to keep things "perfect" according to her standards.

**Both** would benefit from seeing real examples, testimonials from people like them, and clear answers to the "what's the catch?" question that naturally arises with free software.

**Overall Assessment:** The homepage is strong on features and vision, but needs more **proof, reassurance, and specificity** to convert cautious, busy parish leaders into confident users.
